The Battle of the Frontiers

The Battle of the Frontiers were a series battles fought along the eastern frontier of France and in southern Belgium, shortly after the outbreak of the First World War.

The outbreak of the war was especially gruesome as it was the first time in 100 years that the major powers of europe came to blows. A marriage of Napoleanoic Tactics and modern weapons produced a charnal horror house which the world had never seen.

For example, during the inital French push against the Germans, they suffered 27,000 killed in a single day

The Germans sought to execute their Schlieffen plan. they hoped to achieve a swift victory by completely encircling the French army, recreating the feat Hannibal Barca had inflicted on Rome at the battle of Canae.

there would be no swift victory

what resulted was known as the race to the sea. Both armies pushed north in a desperate attempt to out flank each other. neither army got the upper hand, and in the soldiers' desperate attempt to avoid the terrific amount of steel and shrapnel flying above ground, they went underground, forming trenches that remained mostly static throughout the duration of the war.

The Battle of Verdun:

The Charnal Horror House

leading up to the battle of verdun, the Western Front had become static with both sides entrenching themselves. the romantic notions of war were over, and the realities of modern war had set in. battlefields had turned into meat-grinders with generals funneling men into machine gun and artillary fire, the blades of the meat-grinder

The battle of Verdun was initiated by Germans with Operation Gericht, Operation Judgement. Erich von Falkenhayn, The chief architect of the battle of verdun, sought to create an intentional meat-grinder where he would bleed the french white

The French General Robert Nivelle met the German aggression, credited with the rallying cry for the French Ils ne passeront pas, they shall not pass. Verdun became a thermopylae

The Battle of The Somme

The Battle of the Somme represented one of the worst day for the british empire. intended to relieve the french suffering at the battle of Verdun, it saw colossal loss of life. the united kingdom enacted the "pal" enlistment policy which encouraged men to enlist under the promise that they coud serve with their friends. This had the horrific uninteded consequence of Many english towns having their male populations decimated in a single day.

Single days where everyone lost someone

the battle of the Somme saw the introduction of Tanks. The British fielded the mark I tank, the first of its kind. The tank terrified the German defender but deployed in too few numbers it was ultimately ineffective

the Somme, like most other WWI offensives, was a failure. instead of invograting the British Empire forces, it demoralized them. The British general Sir Douglas Haig earned his nickname, the Butcher of the Somme

In the novel, Tender is the Night F. Scott Fitzgeral wrote of the Somme:

"See that little stream — we could walk to it in two minutes. It took the British a month to walk to it — a whole empire walking very slowly, dying in front and pushing forward behind. And another empire walked very slowly backward a few inches a day, leaving the dead like a million bloody rugs. . .there was a century of middle-class love spent here"
